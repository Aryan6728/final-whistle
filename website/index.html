The problem

Settlement is where trust breaks down

Every prediction market eventually asks the same question: who decides the result, and how long do you wait for them to decide it?

How it usually works

  • A backend or admin manually marks a market as resolved
  • Committee-based oracles hold multi-day dispute windows
  • Fast-moving markets — next scorer, next corner — are too quick for manual review
  • Payouts wait on a person, not on the match

How Final Whistle works

  • TxLINE signs match data and anchors it on Solana as it happens
  • Our program calls directly into TxLINE's on-chain validate_stat instruction
  • A confirmed outcome settles the market in the same transaction
  • Payouts wait on the final whistle, not on a resolver

How it works

From stake to settlement

01

Stake on an outcome

Pick a market — match winner or total goals — and lock SOL or USDC into it.

02

Funds sit in escrow

A program-controlled PDA vault holds the pool. No team wallet ever touches it.

03

TxLINE signs the result

When the match ends, TxLINE anchors a signed Merkle proof of the final stat on Solana.

04

The program verifies it

settle_market calls TxLINE's validate_stat instruction directly — no external oracle, no re-implemented crypto.

05

The correct side gets paid

Settlement and payout happen in the same on-chain step the match ends on.
